# Open Erdős Problems

These are famous problems posed by Paul Erdős that remain unsolved.

## Problems

| Problem | Description | Prize |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| [Arithmetic Progressions Conjecture](./erdos-arithmetic-progressions-conjecture/) | Sets with divergent reciprocal sums contain arbitrarily long APs | $5,000 |
| [Erdős–Straus Conjecture](./erdos-straus-conjecture/) | 4/n can always be written as sum of 3 unit fractions | - |
| [Erdős–Turán Conjecture](./erdos-turan-conjecture/) | Additive bases of order 2 have unbounded representation | $3,000 |
| [Erdős–Hajnal Conjecture](./erdos-hajnal-conjecture/) | Forbidden induced subgraphs force polynomial Ramsey | $500 |
| [Erdős–Ulam Problem](./erdos-ulam-problem/) | Is there a dense set in the plane with all rational distances? | - |
| [Erdős–Gyárfás Conjecture](./erdos-gyarfas-conjecture/) | Graphs with min degree 3 have cycles of length 2^k | - |
| [Prime Gaps Conjecture](./erdos-prime-gaps-conjecture/) | Divergence of sum of squared prime gaps | $10,000 |
| [Erdős–Mollin–Walsh Conjecture](./erdos-mollin-walsh-conjecture/) | No three consecutive powerful numbers exist | - |

## About Erdős Problems

Paul Erdős (1913–1996) posed thousands of problems throughout his career, often offering cash prizes for solutions. His problems span combinatorics, graph theory, number theory, and probability. Many remain unsolved and continue to drive mathematical research.
